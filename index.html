1. Global GDP and Population

The worldwide distribution of Gross Domestic Product (GDP) showcases the economic wealth of different countries, allowing for comparing and contrasting GDP levels across regions. The disparities in economic prosperity offer a comprehensive view of the global economic landscape. The distribution of the world's population across different countries and continents showcases population density, highlighting densely populated areas and sparsely inhabited regions. It offers a compelling visual narrative of human settlement patterns, aiding in the understanding of global demographics and urbanization trends.

The economic prosperity of a country (GDP) does not correlates with the amount of waste it generates as wealthier countries produce less waste. However, waste generation scales concerns the population. Larger populations tend to produce more waste due to increased consumption and industrial activities.
